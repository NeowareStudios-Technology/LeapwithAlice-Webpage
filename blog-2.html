Empowering Potential

“From my very first day as an entrepreneur, I’ve felt the only mission worth pursuing in business is to make people’s lives better.” -Richard Branson

We’re all born with incredible potential. It is only once our young brain runs into extrinsic obstacles that we begin to set limitations on ourselves and others. The older we get, the more we hold ourselves back and become set in our ways. At an early age, each of us possess unique learning abilities. Unfortunately, society has not fostered the growth of these educational superpowers. Acceptance of our differences is not enough, we must promote them. Incredibly successful individuals, such as Richard Branson, are paving the way for the next generation of creative thinkers.

“Being different is your biggest asset and will help you succeed.” -Richard Branson

Fulfilling potential is the goal. However, in order to reach that goal, we must first empower that potential. In regards to education, this goes for both the teacher and the student. For students, technology (such as augmented reality) can be the source of individualized learning that targets specific unique learning abilities. Technology can empower teachers, allowing them to educate effectively and create personalized learning experiences. In a recent article, Branson detailed a three step strategy for dyslexic children.

“Being different is your biggest asset and will help you succeed.” -Richard Branson

1. Find your passions and pursue them doggedly.
2. Do your best.
3. Don’t be afraid to ask for help.

We are all best at doing what we love. The challenge in life is finding that passion. With age, we begin to put more experiences behind us. If we are lucky, one of those experiences leads us down a path motivated by passion. Unfortunately, society tends to place limitations on the variety of experiences students have. The proper implementation of technology in the classroom can change that. We can begin to provide educational content that stimulates the mind and engages all senses. Establishing a deeper connection, students will be able to comprehend and retain knowledge more effectively — propelling them towards a future where they are prepared to find their passion.

“Your best is always good enough and the only person you should be comparing yourself to is yourself.” -Richard Branson

Our strongest efforts will always yield the strongest results. However, “our best” is relative to the resources at our disposal. As a society, we have not done our part to provide the most impactful resources to teachers and students. Inadequate pay, scarce funding, and outdated approaches of the education system have placed a cap on the potential of our next generation. If we remove the cap, “our best” becomes even better; especially for those that had previously been held back or neglected, due to their unique learning abilities. Reimagining the way we educate, augmented reality can create an immersive and individualized learning experience that propels every classroom towards a better best.

“We all have different strengths and we should work together to bring these out in each other.” -Richard Branson

Identifying our strengths is first step towards amplifying them. The earlier we detect how students best absorb education, the sooner we can begin to cultivate those specific strengths. Providing content that intensely engages students with every form of learning (auditory, visual, kinesthetic, reading/writing), we can start identifying individual strengths. From there, educators can begin to build content that enhances every student’s learning experience.

“Thankfully, the world is waking up to the fact that dyslexia is just an alternative way of thinking that, with the right support, does not have to hold children back.” -Richard Branson

How does Leap With Alice support this alternative way of thinking? Our approach is to leverage available technology; finally putting educators ahead of the curve, rather than watching them struggle to keep up. Providing a free tool, we empower educators to create engaging and personalized educational content, using augmented reality. Educators can begin to target unique learning abilities, emphasizing the learning methods that best promote growth. Regardless of culture or religion, one shared goal can unite us all: providing a brighter future for the next generation by improving education on an individual level.
